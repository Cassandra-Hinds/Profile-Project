Dangerous Waters

When I was younger being immersed in water is what calmed me but as I grew up it might be what destroys me.

I was walking alone at night again, something my mom urges me not to do. But the grass on my feet and being next to the lake soothes me. Sadness, worry, and frustration dissipate at the sight of ripples and the clear blue substance. Most importantly I don’t have to hide my gift.

I scope the area to make sure no one was there then dived inside. I closed my eyes as my lungs get used to breathing in the water. It was always a discomfort at first, even after doing it for years. When my breathing pattern became normal again and my eyesight underwater became clear, I started to have fun.

Currents zipped past me and circled me. I swam in one and rode like a bull righter, except I controlled the speed and direction.

Forward, backward, left, right, even upside down at the fastest speed I could muster, and even jumped in the air like a dolphin a few times. Soon my strength had depleted, and tiredness overtook me like a tidal wave.

The temptation to sleep here gripped me, but then my ma worried sick if I’m gone when she wakes. So with a groan, I used the last bit of my energy to have the currents pull me back to the shoreline.

As I laid there - getting used to breathing oxygen again - someone gripped me by the arm and yanked me to my feet. The man before me smiled deviously, “You’re coming with me.”

Kill Him

The threat - no promise - of making it happen or else curls around me tight like a python as I sit on my knee in the dark corner of my subject's room. He is sleeping soundly in bed for now until I can figure out my next course of action.

I have to be strategic; there's no way I'm messing up again.

I have one job: make sure my subject dies.

With a cackle I slam his bed into his dresser, knocking over a one-foot heavy glass vase he placed beautifully at the edge. I grin ear to ear as it plummets toward his head. As the vase centimeters away from making an impact one of his angels appeared, yanking him out the way.

I curse, glaring at the guardian angel.

She towers over my subject - who’s now stirred and fully awake - like a mother hen.

“What happened?” he asks getting off the ground.

His eyes widens on the fallen vase on his pillow and his bed against the dresser. He’s totally oblivious to me and my adversary.

Backing away from his bed as if it were a bomb, my subject looks around his room for a cause, anything to explain what just happened. His breathing becomes fast and shallow and his body shook. Oh, I wish I could read his mind.

Calmly his angel comes up behind him and puts her hands on his shoulder. She doesn't have him see her; just to feel her touch and hear her voice. “Shh… It’s ok. Everything’s alright.”

“Not on my watch,” I say.

With a snap, I cack everything I can that’s made out of glass. Then I flickered the light off and on, adding to his paranoia. And for a sweet icing on top, scratched big bold letter on the wall saying, You’re going to die.

Not being able to take it anymore my subject bolted for the door.

His angel snarls down at my triumph, With narrowed fiery eyes she said “You will not succeed.” Then disappears.

I smirk, “We’ll see about that.”

Suicidal Rebellion

Our world was perfect and tranquil. That until is someone opened it up to the kingdom of darkness. From ever since we do not rule on our land and sometimes not even our own lives.

The horrors of evil are now our masters, plummeting our peaceful world to utter chaos and fear. And I for one refuse to bow it.

“Where were you?” my brother snaps as walking into the house.

I gave him a side glance which makes him even more infuriated.

“You know good in well you’re not supposed to out this late. What if you’ve been caught?”

“You’re over-exaggerating.”

He gips hold of my arms in earnest and his brown eyes showed more worry than anger. “You're all have sis, and if you get caught I lose you.”

I smile, “I love you too.”

He sighs heavily, taking me in a hug and kissing the top of my head. He takes his job as my older brother way too seriously sometimes.

I pull away and grin from ear to ear. “I have come back with good news.”

I led the way to our sorry excuse for a kitchen. The furniture only there is are two old chairs around a small table in the center of the room. And I hardly count the rotten-out cabinet and counter-top furniture; we never use it and it’ll probably crumble if we do. Neither do we use the refrigerator; any silverware and food have along with any other possessions considered valuable we hide tuck away in case of break-ins - which happens often around here under our new masters. At least for now.

“I know that smile.” He says pushing in his chair. “Whatever you are concocting, can not change this.” vaguely gesturing all around him.

“Yes, it can. I’ve found out their weakness. If I could get a hold of it I could drive them out. This would mean returning the power to us, reverting our world to what it was meant to be.”

He raises his eyebrow, “How exactly did you come across such news?”

“I might have went somewhere I was not supposed to be.”

“You d- where?”

“By one of the burning dumps.”

No one besides the evil that rules this place is allowed there. It is where they burn and or dump anything they deem a threat. It is forbidden to look for or recover anything that ends up at a burning dump. Most of the time the fear saturated in such a place is enough to drive people away.

“Two of the devils we call masters we arguing over a book that managed to not be burned to ash; saying if anyone we got our hands on it, it’ll be to their ruin. I believe that book holds their weakness.”

My brother shakes his head, “Even attempting to find that book would be a death sentence. You know already know the horrors our masters do to people not trying to exploit their weakness.”

I bit my lip and close my eyes at the rush graphic images. People had been burned alive and tortured mentally, physically, or emotionally and sometimes all at once. Our also masters have the ability to even control our very being from the inside. Torture is their sadistic lust and they never tarry from it.

I press my hand against my head, trying to clear my mind. “But is not worth it? Looking my brother square in eye I continue, “Knowing that you had a chance, a slight chance to turn our world to the state it used to be - to the state should be- are you not willing to risk everything?”

My brother scrunches his face in hesitation.

“We’re going to succumb to death sooner or later by their hands anyway, why not die as a hero instead of a victim?”

He sits back in his chair, with a sigh. I impatiently study his expression until he finally nodded. “Please tell me with this suicide run you have a plan.”

I grin, “I thought you’d never ask.”
